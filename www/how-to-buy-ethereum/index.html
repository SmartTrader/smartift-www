How to Buy Ethereum

If you’ve never purchased Ethereum, or any cryptocurrency for that matter, before – it can be a daunting process. To help those interested in investing in SIFT this guide shows a couple of ways that you can buy ether to fund your investment. It’s important to note that SIFT does not support or endorse any of the websites listed here, we’re merely presenting some of the most popular ways and as always, we’d suggest you feel comfortable doing business with any third partner. If you already hold another cryptocurrency, such as Bitcoin, it is possible to convert your Bitcoin to Ethereum. This involves signing up for an exchange, such as Poloniex or Kraken, obtaining your deposit address for Bitcoin and then trading between them. This technique can be slightly more advanced and so isn’t covered in this guide but it’s an option that may interest you further.

Coinbase

Coinbase is a well-established company that provide a platform to buy and sell crypto currencies. You can fund crypto purchase via your bank account, credit card or debit card depending on your country. You need to start by signing-up at Coinbase. If you use the link provided you’ll get an addition $10 of free cryptocurrency after you’ve bought your first $100.

You’ll then be sent an e-mail to confirm your e-mail address. Once you’ve clicked the link you can complete your process and have to enter a few details – whether you are a business or individual, your phone number and some ID.

You can verify your personal ID with a passport, driving license or other form of photo ID. Once you’ve uploaded it you may have to wait a few minutes for it to validate.

Once you’ve completed sign-up and added a payment method you’ll be taken to the Buy/Sell screen where you can select Ethereum. Here you just select Ethereum, enter how much Ethereum you want to buy, or how much you want to spend in your local currency, and press “Buy Ethereum Instantly”. If funding by a card you’ll then be taken to your card issuer’s confirmation site to confirm the transaction.

Once you’re done you’ve got a balance in Ethereum that you’re ready to withdraw to your wallet for investing in SIFT.

There is a weekly purchase limit on each account. To increase the amount you can buy you may need to provide additional ID – just click the “Increase limits” link on the Buy/Sell screen and provide additional identification.

Other Purchase Sites

Some other popular purchasing sites are cex and Coinhouse. The steps for both of these sites is pretty similar – sign-up, provide some proof of identity and then purchase your Ethereum.

What Next?

Once you’ve got your balance in ether you are ready to transfer it to your own wallet. You cannot fund SIFT directly from an exchange or purchasing site like Coinbase, cex or Coinhouse – so you’ll need a wallet. There are a few ways to do this. The easiest is to use an online wallet but if you want to have all the benefits of a completely secure and local wallet you can try running your own local wallet. Check out our pages on both these setups to learn more.
