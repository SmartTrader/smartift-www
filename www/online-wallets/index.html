Investing in SIFT Using an Online Wallet

To invest in SIFT you need to send our smart contract funds from a wallet. If you send funds from the place your bought your ether (for example Coinbase, cex or an exchange like Kraken) then the way that the network works means that we’ll end up issuing SIFT to them instead of you. Since there is no way to undo such an allocation it’s really important you get your ether into your own wallet. An online wallet is the easiest way to get going. You just go to a website, generate a new Ethereum address and then you can send money from your exchange to your wallet and fund SIFT from there. There are dozens of options for online wallets and you have to have a large amount of trust in your wallet provider – theoretically any online wallet provider could take your credentials and gain access to your address. Whilst we do personally use the site we’ll be showing you in this tutorial we cannot endorse or vouch for any provider. The only way to be completely secure when it comes to your Ethereum is to run your own local wallet.

Creating Your Wallet

MyEtherWallet is one of the most popular online Ethereum wallets. They provide their own guide to get started but hopefully ours will also prove useful. The first thing you will use on the homepage is the ability to create a new wallet. To do this you will need a strong password. We’d highly recommend generating a secure and random password using a website like passwordsgenerator.net with at least a length of 32 characters.

Once you have a password enter it in the “Enter a password” section on the MyEtherWallet homepage then press Create New Wallet.

You will be given an option to download your keystore file. If you do not do this now you may never be able to use your wallet and any funds sent to it may be lost. You need this file, along with the password you just entered, to access your wallet and to send funds. Press the Download button, ensure you have the file saved safely then press “Continue”.

The next page will show you your private key. This is the most secret part of your wallet. With this private key someone can access your wallet even without your password and file that you just downloaded. This should never be kept on your computer. We would advise pressing the Print Paper Wallet button, printing a few copies and putting them in a secure place. If you haven’t got a printer then write down your private key. This is the only way you can access your wallet if you forget your password or lose the file you downloaded.

Press “Save Your Address” then check you can access your wallet. Select “Keystore File”, select the file you downloaded, enter your password and press Unlock. You will then be shown a summary of your Ethereum wallet.

Here we can see that our address is 0x1DaF22790e45C7b7812d03c19f939A388120f2Dc – this is where we will need to send our funds to.

Funding Your Wallet

Depending on where you purchased your ether from you now need to send Ethereum to your new wallet. If you used Coinbase then these steps should walk you through it. Login to your Coinbase account and then click Send/Request in the top. You’ll be shown a form where you need to enter your recipient address and how much to send.

In the recipient address enter the Ethereum wallet address we created in the previous section. In the amount section put how much Ethereum you want to send or press “Use max” to send your full wallet. It’s advisable to first send a small amount – like 0.01 ETH before sending a large amount. This lets you double check you’ve got your account address correct and that your wallet is working. The processes for cex, Coinhouse and other exchanges are all pretty similar. The one thing to note with all of them is that there is a transaction fee that gets paid to keep the Ethereum network running – in the example above it is 0.000441 ETH (or approximately 10 US cents). This fee varies depending on the network’s current state but is never more than a few cents. Since you’ll need a little bit more ether to invest in SIFT than the price per share (as you’ll have another few cents of transaction fee to pay) it’s always worth sending a little bit more of ether – for example if you want to purchase 100 SIFT for 0.01 each we’d recommend you send your wallet 1.01 SIFT just to make sure you have enough left over to cover transaction fees for the foreseeable future.

Investing in SIFT

Once you’ve funded your wallet you should be able to sign back in to MyEtherWallet and see the balance you’ve topped up with. Once you’re happy the balance is there click the “Send Ether & Tokens” link at the top and log back in to your wallet.

Remember that you can only invest in SIFT if you are not a US citizen, resident or are domicilied in the USA - this includes any US territories such as Puerto Rico or US Virgin Islands.

The fields you are filling in here are pretty similar to those that you filled in to get funds in to your wallet. In the To address you need to enter the address of the Smart ICO contract – this is 0xf8Fc0cc97d01A47E0Ba66B167B120A8A0DeAb949. In the Amount to Send section fill our how much ether you want to spend on SIFT shares. Each share costs 0.01 ETH so in this example you could buy 115 share and send 1.15 ETH. To ensure your transaction is successful even in busy periods we recommend setting your gas limit to 400,000. Once you’ve filled in the data click Generate Transaction and then press Send Transaction.

Your transaction will be sent to the SIFT ICO and your SIFT balance will be updated.

Checking your SIFT Balance

Once you’ve sent money to the SIFT contract your SIFT will be issued. To view your SIFT balance (and to send SIFT to others) you need to add SIFT to your wallet. If you’re using MyEtherWallet this is simple – go to the Send Ether & Tokens tab and sign-in. On the right hand side click “Add Custom Token”. In the address enter the SIFT contract 0x8a187d5285d316bcbc9adafc08b51d70a0d8e000. In Token Symbol enter SIFT and for Decimals enter 0 then press Save.

Once it has been checked you will see your SIFT balance next to the token. If you’ve just bought SIFT it may take a few minutes (or even up to an hour depending on the gas price) to appear.

Now that you hold SIFT dividends will be made available on a monthly basis to your account or you can freely trade them once the ICO is over. Congratulations! You’re now an investor in SIFT.
