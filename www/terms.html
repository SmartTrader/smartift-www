Structure and Token Sale

SIFT is a product of Logical Trading Systems Limited. It is not an investment fund nor is it the sole activity of Logical Trading Systems Limited. Owning SIFT refers to being in possession of the private keys for an Ethereum address that has a SIFT balance allocated to it. LTS are not aware of who, as a legal entity, is in possession of such keys. An owner of SIFT may be referred to as a “Shareholder” but it is important to note that they are not a legal shareholder in Logical Trading Systems Limited.

Sending the SIFT initial coin offering (“ICO”) smart contract a value of ETH during the ICO period will allocate a balance of SIFT to the sending address at a rate of 100 SIFT per ETH with a minimum allocation of 1 SIFT and no maximum allocation. You are not purchasing SIFT nor are you investing in LTS as a business but are exchanging one digital token for another.

Once the ICO completes LTS may do one of two things:
1.    Decide to cancel the token sale and return all ETH to the addresses that sent ETH in the first place. In this situation, all Ethereum addresses that sent funds to the ICO contract will have to manually request a withdrawal via the Ethereum network. This will be automatically executed and we will have no access to your ETH balance. If this happens the fund will not trade and all rights relating to holding SIFT will be immediately revoked.
2.    Decide to proceed with the token sale. In this a situation, all ETH is withdrawn gradually and converted in to USD, GBP or EUR fiat currencies. The cumulative sum of all these withdrawals in fiat currency will have 15% deducted from an (the “Operating Cost Fund”) and the remainder will be determined as the initial “Fund Value”. This Fund Value will depend upon exchange rates at the time of conversion and may have a higher or lower value than the ETH that was used originally. The Operating Cost Fund will be used to cover initial costs required for LTS to operate SIFT.
You will be allocated a SIFT balance as soon as funds are sent to the ICO contract but you will not be able to send SIFT to other Ethereum addresses until the ICO has been marked as successfully closed.


Entitlements

Being in possession of SIFT entitles the holder to a share of revenue generated from the crypto-trading activities of LTS. We call this “Profit” but it is important to note how we determine Profit and to understand that this is not the full profit of LTS as an entity but rather a very specific definition.

The amount that each Ethereum address possessing SIFT is entitled to is calculated as follows:
1.    The amount we receive in non-crypto currency at the end of a trading transaction minus the amount we spent at the start of the start of the transaction is “Transaction Gross Profit”
2.    Any taxes and fees related to the transaction (including, but not limited to: exchange fees, transaction fees and capital gains tax) are deducted from the “Transaction Profit” to create the “Transaction Net Profit”
3.    Where (1) was a negative amount (2) will also be negative and this would represent a loss of funds during a trade
4.    The sum of all Transaction Net Profit amounts are added together (including any losses) to determine the “Monthly Trading Profit”
5.    If the Monthly Trading Profit is negative no management fees are changed and no dividends are paid out. This this scenario the Fund Value is decreased by the cumulative Transaction Gross Profit for the month.
6.    If there is a positive Monthly Trading Profit:
    a.    A fee is taken from it and given to LTS. This fee is the lower of 1.5% of the entire Fund Value or 15% of the Monthly Trading Profit. No fee is charged until the cumulative amount of fees that should have been charged exceeds the money allocated to the Operating Cost Fund.
    b.    The remaining value after the fee has been taken is the “Dispersion Amount”
    c.    Half of the Dispersion Amount is added to the previous month’s Fund Value to give the new fund value
    d.    The remaining Dispersion Amount is converted to ETH and send to a smart contract
    e.    This smart contract allocates the ETH between all SIFT owners equally so that the total amount of ETH sent per SIFT is equal to half the Dispersion Amount divided by the total SIFT in circulation

Each owner of SIFT is entitled to sell their ownership of SIFT to any other Ethereum address using whatever payment system they desire. The SIFT token smart contracts permits an amount of SIFT to be allocated to any other address. We envisage that the market value of SIFT will relate to the Fund Value at any moment in time but can make no guarantees of any market for SIFT tokens nor for their value.

Addresses with a SIFT balance are entitled to vote on specific decisions with regards to changes in trading style. These votes are limited to the below activities and the structure of each vote and voting thresholds will be announced with each vote:
1. Taking in further funding
2. Closing the fund down
3. Trading a new pair

We may, at our own discretion, offer other votes to holders of SIFT. These votes will take place via a smart contract on the Ethereum blockchain. Such votes in no way give holders of SIFT any rights to manage the day-to-day activities of LTS nor does it infer any legal voting rights with regards to LTS.
